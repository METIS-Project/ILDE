<p class=Normal1><i><font size=2 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:115%;font-style:italic'>However, in order
to adapt it to this use, the designer / researcher needs to define a protocol
and a set of Heuristics. In a heuristic evaluation, a group of experts (usually
5-7) are asked to “walk through” the evaluated system as if they were users
(learners) engaged in a typical activity. The experts are presented with a set
of design heuristics - “rules of thumb” against which they are asked to assess
their experience. Often they are provided with a score sheet, where they are
asked to note any violation of these heuristics and rate its severity.</span></font></i></p>

should use it to produce the evaluation protocol you will present to your
experts. Create a document from this template, and edit it - replacing the
guidance notes with your own text. The final product should be sufficient to
guide and support an expert in evaluating your project.</span></font></i></p>

<h2><b><font size=4 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB style='font-size:
14.0pt;line-height:115%'><span style='line-height:115%'>Heuristics</span></span></font></b></h2>

<p class=Normal1><font size=2 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:115%'>Below are exemplar four sets of heuristics.
While the overall emphasis is on usability, they all consider factors of
learning design and content design as well. </span></font></p>

<p class=Normal1><font size=2 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:115%'>There is considerable overlap between
these sets, and yet each one includes some unique heuristics. It is also worth
considering the different ways of representing the heuristics to the
evaluators.</span></font></p>

<p class=Normal1><font size=2 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:115%'>You can use one of these sets, pick
and choose from them, or develop your own Heuristics.</span></font></p>

<p class=Normal1><font size=2 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:115%'>&nbsp;</span></font></p>

<h3><b><font size=3 color="#666666" face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'><span style='line-height:115%'>Bealle
and Sharples (2002)</span></span></font></b></h3>

<p class=Normal1><font size=2 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:115%'>(used by the n<i><span
style='font-style:italic'>Quire Moon Rock Demonstrator project, developed for
the Wolfson OpenScience Laboratory)</span></i></span></font></p>

<p class=Normal1><font size=2 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:115%'>&nbsp;</span></font></p>

<p class=Normal1><b><font size=2 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:115%;font-weight:bold'>Feedback</span></font></b><span
lang=EN-GB>: inform the user about what is going on using appropriate feedback
in a timely</span></p>

<p class=Normal1><font size=2 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:115%'>manner.</span></font></p>

<p class=Normal1><b><font size=2 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:115%;font-weight:bold'>Everyday language</span></font></b><span
lang=EN-GB>: use simple language, avoid technical terms, follow real-world
conventions</span></p>

<p class=Normal1><font size=2 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:115%'>to make things appear logical.</span></font></p>

<p class=Normal1><b><font size=2 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:115%;font-weight:bold'>Undo</span></font></b><span
lang=EN-GB>: people make mistakes, so it should be easy to recover to a
sensible point.</span></p>

<p class=Normal1><b><font size=2 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:115%;font-weight:bold'>Consistency</span></font></b><span
lang=EN-GB>: doing similar things in similar places should have similar
effects. Also, support the</span></p>

<p class=Normal1><font size=2 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:115%'>conventions of the specific types of
computer and operating systems such as Windows or</span></font></p>

<p class=Normal1><font size=2 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:115%'>MacOS.</span></font></p>

<p class=Normal1><b><font size=2 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:115%;font-weight:bold'>Recognition not
recall</span></font></b><span lang=EN-GB>: make next steps and critical
information visible and memorable. Allow</span></p>

<p class=Normal1><font size=2 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:115%'>people to recognise what they should
do next, not remember what it is.</span></font></p>

<p class=Normal1><b><font size=2 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:115%;font-weight:bold'>Simple design</span></font></b><span
lang=EN-GB>: keep things crisp and simple, to minimise the information presented
to the user.</span></p>

<p class=Normal1><font size=2 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:115%'>Make the design aesthetically
pleasing to the target audience.</span></font></p>

<p class=Normal1><b><font size=2 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:115%;font-weight:bold'>Expert use</span></font></b><span
lang=EN-GB>: provide accelerators (keyboard short cuts and advanced techniques)
that allow</span></p>

<p class=Normal1><font size=2 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:115%'>experts to work faster.</span></font></p>

<p class=Normal1><b><font size=2 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:115%;font-weight:bold'>Error recovery</span></font></b><span
lang=EN-GB>: try and design the system to prevent errors occurring, and when
they do</span></p>

<p class=Normal1><font size=2 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:115%'>provide clear messages and suggest
appropriate solutions.</span></font></p>

<p class=Normal1><b><font size=2 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:115%;font-weight:bold'>Documentation</span></font></b><span
lang=EN-GB>: it is best to design a system that requires no documentation, but
complex</span></p>

<p class=Normal1><font size=2 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:115%'>features or very different systems
may need it. It should be well organised, (searchable and</span></font></p>

<p class=Normal1><font size=2 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:115%'>well structured), focussed on the
task of the user, simple to follow with concrete steps, and</span></font></p>

<p class=Normal1><font size=2 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:115%'>concise. It should ideally be
available on the system so it is accessible when needed.</span></font></p>

<h3><b><font size=3 color="#666666" face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'><span style='line-height:115%'>Ssemugabi
&amp; de Villiers (2010)</span></span></font></b></h3>

<p class=Normal1><b><font size=2 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:115%;font-weight:bold'>Category 1: General
interface usability criteria (based on Nielsen’s heuristics, modified for
e-learning context)</span></font></b></p>

<p class=Normal1><b><font size=2 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:115%;font-weight:bold'>1 Visibility of
system status</span></font></b></p>

<p class=Normal1 style='margin-left:36.0pt;text-indent:-18.0pt'><font size=2
color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:
115%'>●<font size=1 face="Times New Roman"><span style='font:7.0pt "Times New Roman"'>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
</span></font></span></font><span lang=EN-GB>The website keeps the user
informed through constructive, appropriate and timely feedback.</span></p>

<p class=Normal1 style='margin-left:36.0pt;text-indent:-18.0pt'><font size=2
color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:
115%'>●<font size=1 face="Times New Roman"><span style='font:7.0pt "Times New Roman"'>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
</span></font></span></font><span lang=EN-GB>The system responds to
user-initiated actions. There are no surprise actions by the site or tedious
data entry sequences.</span></p>

<p class=Normal1><b><font size=2 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:115%;font-weight:bold'>2 Match between the
system and the real world i.e. match between designer model and user model</span></font></b></p>

<p class=Normal1 style='margin-left:36.0pt;text-indent:-18.0pt'><font size=2
color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:
115%'>●<font size=1 face="Times New Roman"><span style='font:7.0pt "Times New Roman"'>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
</span></font></span></font><span lang=EN-GB>Language usage in terms of
phrases, symbols, and concepts is similar to that of users in their day-to-day
environment.</span></p>

<p class=Normal1 style='margin-left:36.0pt;text-indent:-18.0pt'><font size=2
color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:
115%'>●<font size=1 face="Times New Roman"><span style='font:7.0pt "Times New Roman"'>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
</span></font></span></font><span lang=EN-GB>Metaphor usage corresponds to
real-world objects/concepts, e.g. understandable and meaningful symbolic
representations are used to ensure that the symbols, icons and names are
intuitive within the context of the task</span></p>

<p class=Normal1 style='margin-left:36.0pt;text-indent:-18.0pt'><font size=2
color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:
115%'>●<font size=1 face="Times New Roman"><span style='font:7.0pt "Times New Roman"'>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
</span></font></span></font><span lang=EN-GB>performed.</span></p>

<p class=Normal1 style='margin-left:36.0pt;text-indent:-18.0pt'><font size=2
color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:
115%'>●<font size=1 face="Times New Roman"><span style='font:7.0pt "Times New Roman"'>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
</span></font></span></font><span lang=EN-GB>Information is arranged in a
natural and logical order.</span></p>

<p class=Normal1><b><font size=2 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:115%;font-weight:bold'>3 Learner control
and freedom</span></font></b></p>

<p class=Normal1 style='margin-left:36.0pt;text-indent:-18.0pt'><font size=2
color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:
115%'>●<font size=1 face="Times New Roman"><span style='font:7.0pt "Times New Roman"'>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
</span></font></span></font><span lang=EN-GB>Users control the system.</span></p>

<p class=Normal1 style='margin-left:36.0pt;text-indent:-18.0pt'><font size=2
color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:
115%'>●<font size=1 face="Times New Roman"><span style='font:7.0pt "Times New Roman"'>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
</span></font></span></font><span lang=EN-GB>Users can exit the system at any
time, even when they have made mistakes.</span></p>

<p class=Normal1 style='margin-left:36.0pt;text-indent:-18.0pt'><font size=2
color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:
115%'>●<font size=1 face="Times New Roman"><span style='font:7.0pt "Times New Roman"'>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
</span></font></span></font><span lang=EN-GB>There are facilities for Undo and
Redo.</span></p>

<p class=Normal1><b><font size=2 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:115%;font-weight:bold'>4 Consistency and
adherence to standards</span></font></b></p>

<p class=Normal1 style='margin-left:36.0pt;text-indent:-18.0pt'><font size=2
color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:
115%'>●<font size=1 face="Times New Roman"><span style='font:7.0pt "Times New Roman"'>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
</span></font></span></font><span lang=EN-GB>The same concepts, words, symbols,
situations, or actions refer to the same thing.</span></p>

<p class=Normal1 style='margin-left:36.0pt;text-indent:-18.0pt'><font size=2
color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:
115%'>●<font size=1 face="Times New Roman"><span style='font:7.0pt "Times New Roman"'>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
</span></font></span></font><span lang=EN-GB>Common platform standards are
followed.</span></p>

<p class=Normal1><b><font size=2 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:115%;font-weight:bold'>5 Error prevention,
in particular, prevention of peripheral usability-related errors</span></font></b></p>

<p class=Normal1 style='margin-left:36.0pt;text-indent:-18.0pt'><font size=2
color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:
115%'>●<font size=1 face="Times New Roman"><span style='font:7.0pt "Times New Roman"'>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
</span></font></span></font><span lang=EN-GB>The system is designed in such a way
that the users cannot easily make serious errors.</span></p>

<p class=Normal1 style='margin-left:36.0pt;text-indent:-18.0pt'><font size=2
color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:
115%'>●<font size=1 face="Times New Roman"><span style='font:7.0pt "Times New Roman"'>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
</span></font></span></font><span lang=EN-GB>When a user makes an error, the
application gives an appropriate error message.</span></p>

<p class=Normal1><b><font size=2 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:115%;font-weight:bold'>6 Recognition rather
than recall</span></font></b></p>

<p class=Normal1 style='margin-left:36.0pt;text-indent:-18.0pt'><font size=2
color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:
115%'>●<font size=1 face="Times New Roman"><span style='font:7.0pt "Times New Roman"'>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
</span></font></span></font><span lang=EN-GB>Objects to be manipulated, options
for selection, and actions to be taken are visible.</span></p>

<p class=Normal1 style='margin-left:36.0pt;text-indent:-18.0pt'><font size=2
color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:
115%'>●<font size=1 face="Times New Roman"><span style='font:7.0pt "Times New Roman"'>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
</span></font></span></font><span lang=EN-GB>The user does not need to recall
information from one part of a dialogue to another.</span></p>

<p class=Normal1 style='margin-left:36.0pt;text-indent:-18.0pt'><font size=2
color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:
115%'>●<font size=1 face="Times New Roman"><span style='font:7.0pt "Times New Roman"'>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
</span></font></span></font><span lang=EN-GB>Instructions on how to use the
system are visible or easily retrievable whenever appropriate.</span></p>

<p class=Normal1 style='margin-left:36.0pt;text-indent:-18.0pt'><font size=2
color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:
115%'>●<font size=1 face="Times New Roman"><span style='font:7.0pt "Times New Roman"'>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
</span></font></span></font><span lang=EN-GB>Displays are simple and multiple
page displays are minimised.</span></p>

<p class=Normal1><b><font size=2 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:115%;font-weight:bold'>7 Flexibility and
efficiency of use</span></font></b></p>

<p class=Normal1 style='margin-left:36.0pt;text-indent:-18.0pt'><font size=2
color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:
115%'>●<font size=1 face="Times New Roman"><span style='font:7.0pt "Times New Roman"'>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
</span></font></span></font><span lang=EN-GB>The site caters for different
levels of users, from novice to expert.</span></p>

<p class=Normal1 style='margin-left:36.0pt;text-indent:-18.0pt'><font size=2
color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:
115%'>●<font size=1 face="Times New Roman"><span style='font:7.0pt "Times New Roman"'>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
</span></font></span></font><span lang=EN-GB>Shortcuts or accelerators, unseen
by novice users, are provided to speed up interaction and task completion by
frequent users.</span></p>

<p class=Normal1 style='margin-left:36.0pt;text-indent:-18.0pt'><font size=2

<h3><b><font size=3 color="#666666" face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'><span style='line-height:115%'>Benson
et al (2001)</span></span></font></b></h3>

<h2><b><font size=3 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB style='font-size:
12.0pt;line-height:115%;background:white'>1. <u>Visibility of system status</u>:
The e-learning program keeps the learner informed about what is happening, through
appropriate feedback within reasonable time.</span></font></b></h2>

<p class=Normal1><font size=2 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:115%;background:white'>Sample questions to
ask yourself:</span></font></p>

<p class=Normal1><font size=2 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:115%;background:white'>a. Does the learner
know where they are at all times, how they got there, and how to get back to
the point from which they started?</span></font></p>

<p class=Normal1><font size=2 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:115%;background:white'>b. When modules and
other components of the e-learning (e.g., streaming video) are loading, is the
status of the upload communicated clearly?</span></font></p>

<p class=Normal1><font size=2 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:115%;background:white'>c. Does the learner
have confidence that the e-learning program is operating the way it was
designed to operate?</span></font></p>

<h2><b><font size=3 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB style='font-size:
12.0pt;line-height:115%;background:white'>2. <u>Match between system and the
real world</u>: The e-learning programís interface employs words, phrases and
concepts familiar to the learner, rather than system-oriented terms. Wherever
possible, the e-learning program utilizes real-world conventions that make
information appear in a natural and logical order.</span></font></b></h2>

<p class=Normal1><font size=2 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:115%;background:white'>Sample questions to
ask yourself:</span></font></p>

<p class=Normal1><font size=2 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:115%;background:white'>a. Does the
e-learning programís navigation and interactive design utilize metaphors that
are familiar to the learner either in terms of traditional learning
environments (e.g., lectures, quizzes, etc.) or in terms related to the
specific content of the program?</span></font></p>

<p class=Normal1><font size=2 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:115%;background:white'>b. Is the cognitive
load of the interface as low as possible to enable learners to engage with the
content, tasks, and problems as quickly as possible?</span></font></p>

<p class=Normal1><font size=2 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:115%;background:white'>c. Does the
e-learning program adhere to good principles of human information processing?</span></font></p>

<h2><b><font size=3 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB style='font-size:
12.0pt;line-height:115%;background:white'>3. <u>User control and freedom</u>:
The e-learning program allows the learner to recover from input mistakes and
provides a clearly marked ìemergency exitî to leave an unwanted state without
having to go through an extended dialogue.</span></font></b></h2>

<p class=Normal1><font size=2 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:115%;background:white'>Sample questions to
ask yourself:</span></font></p>

<p class=Normal1><font size=2 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:115%;background:white'>a. Does the
e-learning program allow the learner to move around in the program in an
unambiguous manner, including the capability to go back and review previous
sections?</span></font></p>

<p class=Normal1><font size=2 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:115%;background:white'>b. Does the
e-learning program allow the learner to leave whenever desired, but easily
return to the closest logical point in the program?</span></font></p>

<p class=Normal1><font size=2 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:115%;background:white'>c. Does the
e-learning program distinguish between input errors and cognitive errors,
allowing easy recovery from the former always, and from the latter when it is
pedagogically appropriate?</span></font></p>

<h2><b><font size=3 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB style='font-size:
12.0pt;line-height:115%;background:white'>4. <u>Consistency and standards</u>:
The e-learning program is consistent in its use of different words, situations,
or actions and it adheres to general software and platform conventions.</span></font></b></h2>

<p class=Normal1><font size=2 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:115%;background:white'>Sample questions to
ask yourself:</span></font></p>

<p class=Normal1><font size=2 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:115%;background:white'>a. Does the
e-learning program function properly as long as the computerís screen
resolution, memory allocations, bandwidth, browsers, plug-ins, and other
technical aspects meet the required specifications?</span></font></p>

<p class=Normal1><font size=2 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:115%;background:white'>b. Does the
e-learning program include interactions that are counter-intuitive with respect
to common software conventions?</span></font></p>

<p class=Normal1><font size=2 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:115%;background:white'>c. Does the
e-learning product adhere to widely recognized standards for interactions
(e.g., going back in a Web browser)?</span></font></p>

<h2><b><font size=3 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB style='font-size:
12.0pt;line-height:115%;background:white'>5. <u>Error prevention</u>: The
e-learning program is carefully designed to prevent common problems from
occurring in the first place.</span></font></b></h2>

<p class=Normal1><font size=2 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:115%;background:white'>Sample questions to
ask yourself:</span></font></p>

<p class=Normal1><font size=2 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:115%;background:white'>a. Is the e-learning
program designed so that the learner recognizes when he/she has made a mistake
related to input rather than content?</span></font></p>

<p class=Normal1><font size=2 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:115%;background:white'>b. Is the e-learning
program designed to take advantage of screen design conventions and guidelines
that clarify meaning?</span></font></p>

<p class=Normal1><font size=2 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:115%;background:white'>c. Is the e-learning
program designed to provide a second chance when unexpected input is received
(e.g., ìYou typed ìbatî in response to the question. Did you mean ìtab?î)?</span></font></p>

<h2><b><font size=3 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB style='font-size:
12.0pt;line-height:115%;background:white'>6. <u>Recognition rather than recall</u>:
The e-learning program makes objects, actions, and options visible so that the
user does not have to remember information from one part of the program to
another. Instructions for use of the program are visible or easily retrievable.</span></font></b></h2>

<p class=Normal1><font size=2 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:115%;background:white'>Sample questions to
ask yourself:</span></font></p>

<p class=Normal1><font size=2 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:115%;background:white'>a. Does the
interface of the e-learning program speak for itself so that extensive
consultation of a manual or other documentation does not interfere with
learning?</span></font></p>

<p class=Normal1><font size=2 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:115%;background:white'>b. Are icons and
other screen elements designed so that they are as intuitive as possible?</span></font></p>

<p class=Normal1><font size=2 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:115%;background:white'>c. Does the
e-learning program provide user-friendly hints and/or clear directions when the
learner requests assistance?</span></font></p>

<h2><b><font size=3 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB style='font-size:
12.0pt;line-height:115%;background:white'>7. <u>Flexibility and efficiency of
use</u>: The e-learning program is designed to speed up interactions for the
experienced learner, but also cater to the needs of the inexperienced learner.</span></font></b></h2>

<p class=Normal1><font size=2 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:115%;background:white'>Sample questions to
ask yourself:</span></font></p>

<p class=Normal1><font size=2 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:115%;background:white'>a. Is the e-learning
program designed to make the best use of useful graphics and other media
elements that download as quickly as possible?</span></font></p>

<p class=Normal1><font size=2 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:115%;background:white'>b. Is the e-learning
program designed to allow large media files to be downloaded in advance so that
learner wait time is minimized?</span></font></p>

<p class=Normal1><font size=2 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:115%;background:white'>c. Does the program
allow keyboard short cuts that make frequent interactions as efficient as
possible?</span></font></p>

<h2><b><font size=3 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB style='font-size:
12.0pt;line-height:115%;background:white'>8. <u>Aesthetic and minimalist design</u>:
Screen displays do not contain information that is irrelevant, and ìbells and
whistlesî are not gratuitously added to the e-learning program.</span></font></b></h2>

<p class=Normal1><font size=2 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:115%;background:white'>Sample questions to
ask yourself:</span></font></p>

<p class=Normal1><font size=2 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:115%;background:white'>a. Are the font
choices, colors, and sizes consistent with good screen design recommendations
for e-learning programs?</span></font></p>

<p class=Normal1><font size=2 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:115%;background:white'>b. Are extra media
features (e.g., streaming video) in the e-learning program supportive of
learning, motivation, content, or other goals?</span></font></p>

<p class=Normal1><font size=2 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:115%;background:white'>c. Does the e-learning
program utilize white space and other screen design conventions appropriately?</span></font></p>

<h2><b><font size=3 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB style='font-size:
12.0pt;line-height:115%;background:white'>9. <u>Help users recognize, diagnose,
and recover from errors</u>: The e-learning program expresses error messages in
plain language (without programmer codes), precisely indicates the problem, and
constructively suggests a solution.</span></font></b></h2>

<p class=Normal1><font size=2 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:115%;background:white'>Sample questions to
ask yourself:</span></font></p>

<p class=Normal1><font size=2 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:115%;background:white'>a. Does the learner
receive meaningful feedback concerning the nature of any input they make into
the program?</span></font></p>

<p class=Normal1><font size=2 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:115%;background:white'>b. If the learner
answers a question incorrectly, is he/she told the correct answer and why the
answer given was wrong, if this is instructionally appropriate?</span></font></p>

<p class=Normal1><font size=2 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:115%;background:white'>c. When feedback is
provided, is it given in a clear, direct, and friendly (non-condescending)
manner?</span></font></p>

<h2><b><font size=3 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB style='font-size:
12.0pt;line-height:115%;background:white'>10. <u>Help and documentation</u>:
When it is absolutely necessary to provide help and documentation, the
e-learning program provides any such information in a manner that is easy to
search. Any help provided is focused on the learner's task, lists concrete
steps to be carried out, and is not be too large.</span></font></b></h2>

<p class=Normal1><font size=2 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:115%;background:white'>Sample questions to
ask yourself:</span></font></p>

<p class=Normal1><font size=2 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:115%;background:white'>a. Is help provided
that is screen or context specific?</span></font></p>

<p class=Normal1><font size=2 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:115%;background:white'>b. Is help and
documentation available from any logical part of the e-learning program?</span></font></p>

<p class=Normal1><font size=2 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:115%;background:white'>c. Does the
e-learning program include a map or table of contents that allows you to see
what you have seen and not seen?</span></font></p>

<h2><b><font size=3 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB style='font-size:
12.0pt;line-height:115%;background:white'>11. <u>Interactivity</u>: The
e-learning program provides content-related interactions and tasks that support
meaningful learning.</span></font></b></h2>

<p class=Normal1><font size=2 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:115%;background:white'>Sample questions to
ask yourself:</span></font></p>

<p class=Normal1><font size=2 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:115%;background:white'>a. Does the
e-learning program provide too many long sections of text to read without
meaningful interactions?</span></font></p>

<p class=Normal1><font size=2 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:115%;background:white'>b. Does the
e-learning engage the learner in content-specific tasks to complete and
problems to solve that take advantage of the state-of-the-art of e-learning
design?</span></font></p>

<p class=Normal1><font size=2 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:115%;background:white'>c. Does the
e-learning program provide a level of experiential learning congruent with the
content and capabilities of the target audience?</span></font></p>

<h2><b><font size=3 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB style='font-size:
12.0pt;line-height:115%;background:white'>12. <u>Message Design</u>: The
e-learning program presents information in accord with sound principles of
information-processing theory.</span></font></b></h2>

<p class=Normal1><font size=2 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:115%;background:white'>Sample questions to
ask yourself:</span></font></p>

<p class=Normal1><font size=2 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:115%;background:white'>a. Is the most
important information on the screen placed in the areas most likely to attract
the learnerís attention?</span></font></p>

<p class=Normal1><font size=2 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:115%;background:white'>b. Does the
e-learning program follow good information presentation guidelines with respect
to organization and layout?</span></font></p>

<p class=Normal1><font size=2 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:115%;background:white'>c. Are graphics in
the e-learning program used to clarify content, motivate, or serve other
pedagogical goals?</span></font></p>

<h2><b><font size=3 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB style='font-size:
12.0pt;line-height:115%;background:white'>13. <u>Learning Design</u>: The

principles of learning theory.</span></font></b></h2>

<p class=Normal1><font size=2 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:115%;background:white'>Sample questions to
ask yourself:</span></font></p>

<p class=Normal1><font size=2 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:115%;background:white'>a. Does the
e-learning program provide for instructional interactions that reflect sound
learning theory?</span></font></p>

<p class=Normal1><font size=2 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:115%;background:white'>b. Does the
e-learning program engage learners in tasks that are closely aligned with the
learning goals and objectives?</span></font></p>

<p class=Normal1><font size=2 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:115%;background:white'>c. Does the
e-learning program inform learners of the objectives of the program and remind
them of prior learning?</span></font></p>

<h2><b><font size=3 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB style='font-size:
12.0pt;line-height:115%;background:white'>14. <u>Assessment</u>: The e-learning
program provides assessment opportunities that are aligned with the program
objectives and content.</span></font></b></h2>

<p class=Normal1><font size=2 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:115%;background:white'>Sample questions to
ask yourself:</span></font></p>

<p class=Normal1><font size=2 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:115%;background:white'>a. Does the
e-learning program provide opportunities for self-assessments that advance
learner achievement?</span></font></p>

<p class=Normal1><font size=2 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:115%;background:white'>b. If appropriate to

<p class=Normal1><font size=2 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:115%;background:white'>c. Are higher order
assessments (e.g., analysis, synthesis, and evaluation) provided wherever
appropriate rather than lower order assessments (e.g., recall and recognition)?</span></font></p>

<h2><b><font size=3 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB style='font-size:
12.0pt;line-height:115%;background:white'>15. <u>Media Integration</u>: The
inclusion of media in the e-learning program serves clear pedagogical and/or
motivational purposes.</span></font></b></h2>

<p class=Normal1><font size=2 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:115%;background:white'>Sample questions to
ask yourself:</span></font></p>

<p class=Normal1><font size=2 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:115%;background:white'>a. Is media included

<p class=Normal1><font size=2 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB

<p class=Normal1><font size=2 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:115%;background:white'>c. If appropriate to
the context, are various forms media included for remediation and/or
enrichment?</span></font></p>

<h2><b><font size=3 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB style='font-size:
12.0pt;line-height:115%;background:white'>16. <u>Resources</u>: The e-learning
program provides access to all the resources necessary to support effective
learning.</span></font></b></h2>

<p class=Normal1><font size=2 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:115%;background:white'>Sample questions to
ask yourself:</span></font></p>

<p class=Normal1><font size=2 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:115%;background:white'>a. Does the
e-learning program provide access to a range of resources (e.g., examples or
real data archives) appropriate to the learning context?</span></font></p>

<p class=Normal1><font size=2 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:115%;background:white'>b. If the e-learning

<p class=Normal1><font size=2 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:115%;background:white'>c. Are resources
provided in a manner that replicates as closely as possible their availability
and use in the real world?</span></font></p>

<h2><b><font size=3 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB style='font-size:
12.0pt;line-height:115%;background:white'>17. <u>Performance Support Tool</u>s:
The e-learning program provides access to performance support tools that are
relevant to the content and objectives.</span></font></b></h2>

<p class=Normal1><font size=2 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:115%;background:white'>Sample questions to
ask yourself:</span></font></p>

<p class=Normal1><font size=2 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:115%;background:white'>a. Are performance
support tools provided that mimic their access in the real world?</span></font></p>

<p class=Normal1><font size=2 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:115%;background:white'>b. Provided the
context is appropriate, does the e-learning program provide sufficient search
capabilities?</span></font></p>

<p class=Normal1><font size=2 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:115%;background:white'>c. Provided the
context is appropriate, does the e-learning program provide access to peers,
experts, instructors, and other human resources?</span></font></p>

<h2><b><font size=3 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB style='font-size:
12.0pt;line-height:115%;background:white'>18. <u>Learning Management</u>: The
e-learning program enables learners to monitor their progress through the
material.</span></font></b></h2>

<p class=Normal1><font size=2 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:115%;background:white'>Sample questions to
ask yourself:</span></font></p>

<p class=Normal1><font size=2 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:115%;background:white'>a. Does the learner
know what he/she is doing and how he/she is doing within various parts of the
e-learning program?</span></font></p>

<p class=Normal1><font size=2 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:115%;background:white'>b. Does the learner
perceive options for additional guidance, instruction, or other forms of
assistance when it is needed?</span></font></p>

<p class=Normal1><font size=2 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:115%;background:white'>c. Does the learner
possess an adequate understanding of what he/she has completed and what remains
to be done within any specific unit (e.g., a course) of e-learning?</span></font></p>

<h2><b><font size=3 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB style='font-size:
12.0pt;line-height:115%;background:white'>19. <u>Feedback</u>: The e-learning
program provides feedback that is contextual and relevant to the problem or
task in which the learner is engaged.</span></font></b></h2>

<p class=Normal1><font size=2 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:115%;background:white'>Sample questions to
ask yourself:</span></font></p>

<p class=Normal1><font size=2 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:115%;background:white'>a. Is the feedback
given at any specific time tailored to the content being studied, problem being
solved, or task being completed by the learner?</span></font></p>

<p class=Normal1><font size=2 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:115%;background:white'>b. Does feedback
provide the learner with information concerning his/her current level of
achievement within the program?</span></font></p>

<p class=Normal1><font size=2 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:115%;background:white'>c. Does the
e-learning program provide learners with opportunities to access extended
feedback from instructors, experts, peers, or others through e-mail or other
Internet communications?</span></font></p>

<h2><b><font size=3 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB style='font-size:
12.0pt;line-height:115%;background:white'>20. <u>Content</u>: The content of
the e-learning program is organized in a manner than is clear to the learner.</span></font></b></h2>

<p class=Normal1><font size=2 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:115%;background:white'>Sample questions to
ask yourself:</span></font></p>

<p class=Normal1><font size=2 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:115%;background:white'>a. Is the content
organized in manageable modules or other types of units?</span></font></p>

<p class=Normal1><font size=2 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:115%;background:white'>b. Is the content
broken to appropriate chunks so that learners can process them without too much
cognitive load?</span></font></p>

<p class=Normal1><font size=2 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:115%;background:white'>c. Does the
e-learning program provides advanced organizers, summaries, and other
components that foster more efficient and effective learning?</span></font></p>
