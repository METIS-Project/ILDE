<h1><a name=h.5yzmzrtpdxrr></a><b><font size=5 color=black face=Arial><span
lang=EN-GB style='font-size:18.0pt;line-height:115%'><span style='line-height:
115%'>Summary</span></span></font></b></h1>

<p class=MsoNormal><i><font size=2 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:115%;font-style:italic'>Write this last.</span></font></i></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-left:36.0pt'><font size=2 color=black
face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:115%'>The <a
href="http://www.ld-grid.org/resources/representations-and-languages/design-patterns"><font
color="#1155cc"><span style='color:#1155CC'>Design patterns</span></font></a>
paradigm (Alexander et al, 1977) was developed as a form of design language
within architecture. This was done with the explicit aim of externalizing
knowledge to allow the accumulation and generalization of solutions and to
allow all members of a community or design group to participate in discussions
relating to design. These patterns were organized into coherent systems called
pattern languages where patterns are related to each other. The original
definition of a design pattern positions it as a high-level specification of a
method of design which specifies the context of discussion, the particulars of
the problem, and how these can be addressed by the designated design
instruments. A pattern has three facets: descriptive, normative, and
communicative. It is an analytic form, used to describe design situations and
solutions; a meta-design tool, used to highlight key issues and dictate a
method of resolving them; and a communicative tool enabling different
communities to discuss design issues and solutions. The core of a design
pattern can be seen as a local functional statement: “for problem P, under
circumstances C, solution S has been known to work”. Design patterns were
described as abstractions of expert knowledge; they generalise from successful
practice without detaching from its context. As such, they offer a two-way
bridge between practice and theory: opening practical wisdom to theoretical
scrutiny and allowing theory to be projected into practice.</span></font></p>

<h2><a name=h.cp8dltpujsvm></a><b><font size=4 color=black face=Arial><span

<p class=MsoNormal><i><font size=2 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB

<h2><a name=h.anily5xl7q6u></a><b><font size=4 color=black face=Arial><span
lang=EN-GB style='font-size:14.0pt;line-height:115%'><span style='line-height:

<p class=MsoNormal><i><font size=2 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:115%;font-style:italic'>The objectives and
constraints constituting the problem. Often the complexity of noteworthy
problems emerges from a tension between forces: objectives which seem to
contradict, or are incompatible with the constraints.</span></font></i></p>

<h1><a name=h.rp2ab0xev1s0></a><b><font size=5 color=red face=Arial><span
lang=EN-GB style='font-size:18.0pt;line-height:115%;color:red'><span

<h2><a name=h.7q6ee1gpwilr></a><b><font size=4 color=black face=Arial><span
lang=EN-GB style='font-size:14.0pt;line-height:115%'><span style='line-height:
115%'>Origins</span></span></font></b></h2>

<p class=MsoNormal><i><font size=2 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:115%;font-style:italic'>The origins of a
pattern define its context in the most reliable manner: the characterisation of
the common features of the situation descriptions of the design narratives from
which it was derived.</span></font></i></p>

<h3><a name=h.1wp2y2y2ho32></a><b><font size=3 color="#666666" face=Arial><span
lang=EN-GB style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'><span style='line-height:
115%'>Material</span></span></font></b></h3>

<p class=MsoNormal><i><font size=2 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:115%;font-style:italic'>What are the
spatial characteristics of the settings? What tools and resources are available
to protagonists?</span></font></i></p>

<h3><a name=h.te3ka1pdhqtq></a><b><font size=3 color="#666666" face=Arial><span
lang=EN-GB style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'><span style='line-height:

<p class=MsoNormal><i><font size=2 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:115%;font-style:italic'>Who are the
protagonists, what are the relationships between them, and what are the social
and institutional configurations in which they operate?</span></font></i></p>

<h3><a name=h.lwpuy0475aas></a><b><font size=3 color="#666666" face=Arial><span
lang=EN-GB style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'><span style='line-height:

<p class=MsoNormal><i><font size=2 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:115%;font-style:italic'>What are the
protagonists beliefs, desires and intentions, which shape the problem space?</span></font></i></p>

<h3><a name=h.a1i7nvu5jrof></a><b><font size=3 color="#666666" face=Arial><span
lang=EN-GB style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:115%'><span style='line-height:
115%'>Force Map</span></span></font></b></h3>

<p class=MsoNormal><i><font size=2 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:115%;font-style:italic'>A</span></font></i><span
lang=EN-GB><a
href="http://www.ld-grid.org/resources/representations-and-languages/force-map"><i><font
color=black><span style='color:black;font-style:italic;text-decoration:none'> </span></font></i></a><a
href="http://www.ld-grid.org/resources/representations-and-languages/force-map"><i><font
color="#1155cc"><span style='color:#1155CC;font-style:italic'>Force Map</span></font></i></a><i><span
style='font-style:italic'> is a graphical representation of the context of a
design challenge. It includes iconic representations of the key elements in
this context (social, material and intentional factors), and lines noting the
relationships between them. These relationships are marked &quot;+&quot; when
supportive, and &quot;-&quot; when indicating a tension. The design challenge
can often be defined in terms of resolving some of these tensions.</span></i></span></p>

<h2><a name=h.vtg4rospuxek></a><b><font size=4 color=black face=Arial><span

<p class=MsoNormal><i><font size=2 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:115%;font-style:italic'>Based on the
analysis of the pattern, it may be justifiable to claim a broader scope of

application, and should be noted with caution.</span></font></i></p>

<h2><a name=h.5zeefr78fftl></a><b><font size=4 color=black face=Arial><span
lang=EN-GB style='font-size:14.0pt;line-height:115%'><span style='line-height:

<p class=MsoNormal><i><font size=2 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:115%;font-style:italic'>Specifying the
boundaries of a pattern – where it does not apply – is arguably as important as
where it does. These boundaries need to be clearly marked where there is a risk
that a pattern would be applied erroneously. </span></font></i></p>

<h1><a name=h.b11t0notn161></a><b><font size=5 color=red face=Arial><span

<p class=MsoNormal><i><font size=2 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB

<h2><a name=h.hlq0gnp1dent></a><b><font size=4 color=black face=Arial><span
lang=EN-GB style='font-size:14.0pt;line-height:115%'><span style='line-height:

<p class=MsoNormal><i><font size=2 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB

<h2><a name=h.msqzbxn2gyl5></a><b><font size=4 color=black face=Arial><span

<p class=MsoNormal><i><font size=2 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:115%;font-style:italic'>When appropriate, a
diagram should be added to elucidate the solution. In contrast to the
illustration, which communicates at an intuitive or metaphoric level, the
diagram is like a blue-print for implementation.</span></font></i></p>

<h1><a name=h.51pfvpt0cvxo></a><b><font size=5 color=black face=Arial><span

<p class=MsoNormal><font size=2 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB

<h2><a name=h.ud05wn3ltiec></a><b><font size=4 color=black face=Arial><span

<h1><a name=h.afob0c2xdwg0></a><b><font size=5 color=black face=Arial><span

115%'>Support</span></span></font></b></h1>

<p class=MsoNormal><i><font size=2 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:115%;font-style:italic'>If the solution is
the claim, then the support section is its theoretical and empirical

equivalent would be the “known uses” section. The latter is however more in way
of demonstration than evidence.</span></font></i></p>

<h2><a name=h.3r5o70epulpr></a><b><font size=4 color=black face=Arial><span

<p class=MsoNormal><i><font size=2 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:115%;font-style:italic'>Theoretical

<h2><a name=h.5jeho3lb4y4></a><b><font size=4 color=black face=Arial><span
lang=EN-GB style='font-size:14.0pt;line-height:115%'><span style='line-height:
115%'>Empirical</span></span></font></b></h2>

<p class=MsoNormal><i><font size=2 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:115%;font-style:italic'>(This was your
examples section).</span></font></i></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><i><font size=2 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:115%;font-style:italic'>Empirical
calibration justifies the pattern by reference to data from cases where it
appears to have had a positive effect. These would primarily be derived from
the design experiment at hand and supplemented by external documented cases.</span></font></i></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><font size=2 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB

<h1><a name=h.llnc1xxae0iw></a><b><font size=5 color=black face=Arial><span
lang=EN-GB style='font-size:18.0pt;line-height:115%'><span style='line-height:
115%'>Liabilities</span></span></font></b></h1>

<p class=MsoNormal><font size=2 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:115%'>Liabilities are the potential
negative effects and points of failure associated with a pattern.</span></font></p>

<h2><a name=h.sp85snqyl3kc></a><b><font size=4 color=black face=Arial><span
lang=EN-GB style='font-size:14.0pt;line-height:115%'><span style='line-height:

<p class=MsoNormal><font size=2 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:115%'>Risks refer to possible direct
negative side effects. These need to be averted, often by applying a related
pattern.</span></font></p>

<h2><a name=h.wk2dwgj9c013></a><b><font size=4 color=black face=Arial><span

<p class=MsoNormal><font size=2 color=black face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:11.0pt;line-height:115%'>Limitations note the patterns

<h1><a name=h.yi81108dzb7m></a><b><font size=5 color=black face=Arial><span
