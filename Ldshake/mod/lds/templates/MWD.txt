<p class=MsoTitle><a name="_Toc355954521"><b><font size=5 face=Arial><span
lang=EN-GB style='font-size:16.0pt'>METIS Workshop Design (version 0.7)</span></font></b></a></p>

<p class=MsoSubtitle><a name="_Toc355954522"><i><font size=3 face=Arial><span
lang=EN-GB style='font-size:12.0pt;font-style:italic'>Summary</span></font></i></a></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><font size=2 face=Arial><span
lang=EN-US style='font-size:11.0pt'>Despite the abundance of high-quality
educational technology, and the wealth of research demonstrating its value,
educators are still struggling to make effective use of these technologies and
the associated pedagogies in their daily practices. Arguably, this is a gap in
the capacity for learning design: educators need the tools and competencies
which would allow them to identify educational challenges, describe the context
in which they arise, identify the opportunities afforded by technology, project
the insights derived from research, and devise new learning experiences. To
address this gap, educators need tools and practices. Tools that would support
them through the cycle of learning design – from conception to deployment and
evaluation of techno-educational innovations. Professional practices that use
such tools to ensure the robustness and effectiveness of their innovations and
make learning design a daily habit and part of their professional identity. The
METIS project (</span><span lang=EN-GB><a href="http://metis-project.org/"><span
lang=EN-US>http://metis-project.org/</span></a></span></font><span lang=EN-US>)
aims to contribute to this aim, by providing educators with an Integrated
Learning Design Environment (ILDE) and a workshop package for training
educators in using the ILDE to support effective learning design.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><font size=2 face=Arial><span
lang=EN-GB style='font-size:11.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></font></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><font size=2 face=Arial><span
lang=EN-GB style='font-size:11.0pt'>This document describes the aims,
organisational requirements and activity structure for METIS learning design
workshops. The aims are specified in terms of outcomes for participants and the
METIS project. The organisational requirements describe the human and other
resources required to run a workshop. The activity structure describes a
reusable structure of activities to enable participants to reach the learning
outcomes specified. It includes a description of the tools and the information
resources for both participants and facilitators for each activity.</span></font></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><font size=2 face=Arial><span
lang=EN-GB style='font-size:11.0pt'>Our research of user concerns suggests that
practitioners have limited interest in training on learning design in general,
but are have much higher interest in learning design for specific themes, such
as collaborative learning, formative assessment or project based learning.
Hence, the workshop design presented here is a meta-design that is flexible and
can be customised to a specific theme. In this document, this theme is noted as
‘X’. </span></font></p>

learning design is intended to be applicable across a range of topics.</span></font></p>

<h2><a name="_Toc355954527"><b><font size=3 face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:12.0pt'>2.2<font size=1 face="Times New Roman"><span
style='font:7.0pt "Times New Roman"'>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; </span></font></span></font></b><font
size=3><span lang=EN-GB style='font-size:12.0pt'>People: Participants,
facilitators and others</span></font></a></h2>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><font size=2 face=Arial><span
lang=EN-GB style='font-size:11.0pt'>Participants work in teams composed of 3 to
6 members. This team size encourages and facilitates each team member to be
fully involved in design discussions throughout the workshop. A team size of 3
to 6 members is optimal because it allows for a diverse range of views to be
debated. Teams of more than 6 often split up into smaller teams and lose focus;
this  should be avoided if possible. </span></font></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><font size=2 face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB style='font-size:
11.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></font></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><font size=2 face=Arial><span
lang=EN-GB style='font-size:11.0pt'>It is highly advisable to allocate a ‘<i><span
style='font-style:italic'>critical friend’</span></i> to each team to challenge
design thinking and stimulate focused and informed discussion </span></font><span
lang=EN-GB>(</span><span lang=EN-GB><a href="#_ENREF_1" title="Cross, 2012 #550"><font
color=black><span style='color:windowtext;text-decoration:none'>Cross, et al.,
2012, p. 28</span></font></a></span><span lang=EN-GB>)</span><span lang=EN-GB>.
This ‘critical friend’ role could be played by the workshop facilitators
circulating amongst the teams, but if there are many more teams than
facilitators it is advisable to recruit additional ‘critical friends’ to ensure
that each team can benefit from their input and challenges.  A learning design
workshop <i><span style='font-style:italic'>facilitator</span></i> must have
detailed knowledge of the both the workshop topic and the Integrated&nbsp;<b><span
style='font-weight:bold'>Learning Design</span></b>&nbsp;Environment (ILDE) so he/she
can answer any question that might arise during the workshop. A distinction
needs to be made between a <i><span style='font-style:italic'>critical friend</span></i>
and a <i><span style='font-style:italic'>facilitator</span></i>. A <i><span
style='font-style:italic'>critical friend </span></i>is required to have
knowledge of the topic in order to prompt and encourage a focused and robust
discussion. Obviously, some awareness of the ILDE will be useful for <i><span
style='font-style:italic'>critical friends</span></i>. A learning design
workshop <i><span style='font-style:italic'>facilitator</span></i> is an expert
on both the topic and the ILDE. </span></p>

<h2><a name="_Toc355954528"><b><font size=3 face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:12.0pt'>2.3<font size=1 face="Times New Roman"><span
style='font:7.0pt "Times New Roman"'>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; </span></font></span></font></b><font
size=3><span lang=EN-GB style='font-size:12.0pt'>Resources</span></font></a></h2>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><font size=2 face=Arial><span
lang=EN-GB style='font-size:11.0pt'>Each team needs access to one or more
laptop or desktop computers with internet access to interact with the ILDE and
other online resources. Prior to the workshop starting, an empty learning design
for each team will be created in the ILDE, and all team members will be given
editing rights for that learning design. If other resources such as printed
material and specific online resources are required for a particular activity,
they are described within the relevant activity in the activity structure that
follows. </span></font></p>

<h1><a name="_Toc355954531"><b><font size=4 face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB

<p class=MsoNormal><font size=2 face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB style='font-size:

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><font size=2 face=Arial><span

proficient in during the learning activity they are collaboratively designing. These
need to be described in the learning outcomes view.</span></font></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><font size=2 face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB style='font-size:
11.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></font></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><font size=2 face=Arial><span
lang=EN-GB style='font-size:11.0pt'>In this activity the learners are shown a
set of ‘X’ learning patterns. The examples used in the “5 Evidence and examples
of ‘X’” activity will provide concrete examples of some or all of the patterns
that are now made available to the participants.  </span></font></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><font size=2 face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB style='font-size:
11.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></font></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><font size=2 face=Arial><span
lang=EN-GB style='font-size:11.0pt'>Each team will select one or more patterns
which best suit their articulated Vision as defined by the Course Map, Learning
Outcomes view, Personas and the  barriers and challenges that  they developed
during the preceding activities.  If more than one pattern is chosen it could
be because there are alternatives, or because the team thinks that a sequence
of ‘X’ activities is necessary.</span></font></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><font size=2 face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB style='font-size:
11.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></font></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><font size=2 face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB style='font-size:
11.0pt'>They now begin the detailed design. Questions that should be used as
prompts for this session include:</span></font></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><font size=2 face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB style='font-size:
11.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></font></p>

<p class=MsoListParagraphCxSpFirst style='text-indent:-18.0pt'><font size=2
face=Symbol><span lang=EN-GB style='font-size:11.0pt;font-family:Symbol'>·<font
size=1 face="Times New Roman"><span style='font:7.0pt "Times New Roman"'>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
</span></font></span></font><span lang=EN-GB>Which parts of the activity should
be synchronous, and which should be asynchronous?</span></p>

<p class=MsoListParagraphCxSpLast style='text-indent:-18.0pt'><font size=2
face=Symbol><span lang=EN-GB style='font-size:11.0pt;font-family:Symbol'>·<font
size=1 face="Times New Roman"><span style='font:7.0pt "Times New Roman"'>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
</span></font></span></font><span lang=EN-GB>Which tools have the right
affordances for your activity?</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><font size=2 face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB style='font-size:
11.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></font></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><font size=2 face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB style='font-size:
11.0pt'>This could be carried out further using a Think-Pair-Share activity.</span></font></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><font size=2 face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB style='font-size:
11.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></font></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><b><font size=2 face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:11.0pt;font-weight:bold'>Resources</span></font></b></p>

<p class=MsoListParagraph style='text-indent:-18.0pt'><font size=2
face=Wingdings><span lang=EN-GB style='font-size:11.0pt;font-family:Wingdings'>ü<font
size=1 face="Times New Roman"><span style='font:7.0pt "Times New Roman"'>&nbsp;
</span></font></span></font><span lang=EN-GB>A set of design patterns for X. 
These should be provided both online and in printed form.</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><font size=2 face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB style='font-size:
11.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></font></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><b><font size=2 face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:11.0pt;font-weight:bold'>Output</span></font></b></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><font size=2 face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB style='font-size:
11.0pt'>The selected pattern(s), a set of resources that are to be used within
the activity and an annotated diagram of the pattern(s) showing:</span></font></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><font size=2 face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB style='font-size:
11.0pt'> </span></font></p>

<p class=MsoListParagraphCxSpFirst style='text-indent:-18.0pt'><font size=2
face=Symbol><span lang=EN-GB style='font-size:11.0pt;font-family:Symbol'>·<font
size=1 face="Times New Roman"><span style='font:7.0pt "Times New Roman"'>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
</span></font></span></font><span lang=EN-GB>asynchronous and synchronous
stages within the design</span></p>

<p class=MsoListParagraphCxSpLast style='text-indent:-18.0pt'><font size=2
face=Symbol><span lang=EN-GB style='font-size:11.0pt;font-family:Symbol'>·<font
size=1 face="Times New Roman"><span style='font:7.0pt "Times New Roman"'>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
</span></font></span></font><span lang=EN-GB>where particular tools and
resources are used.  </span></p>

<h1><a name="_Toc355954537"><b><font size=4 face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:14.0pt'>8<font size=1 face="Times New Roman"><span
style='font:7.0pt "Times New Roman"'>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; </span></font></span></font></b><font
size=4><span lang=EN-GB style='font-size:14.0pt'>Prototype (1.5 hour)</span></font></a></h1>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><font size=2 face=Arial><span
lang=EN-GB style='font-size:11.0pt'>Participants work together to produce a
prototype of an ‘X’ activity. The prototypes illustrate tasks the facilitator
and learners may choose to undertake. Prototyping would use a suitable ILDE
tool or be carried out using the technology of the team’s choice (including
paper).</span></font></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><font size=2 face=Arial><span
lang=EN-GB style='font-size:11.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></font></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><b><font size=2 face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:11.0pt;font-weight:bold'>Resources</span></font></b></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><font size=2 face=Arial><span
lang=EN-GB style='font-size:11.0pt;font-style:italic'>Participants’ resources:
a guide to the affordances of the ILDE instantiation and deployment tools which
explains which tools may be best suited to instantiate and run patterns of type
X.</span></font></i></p>

<h1><a name="_Toc355954538"><b><font size=4 face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:14.0pt'>9<font size=1 face="Times New Roman"><span
style='font:7.0pt "Times New Roman"'>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; </span></font></span></font></b><font
size=4><span lang=EN-GB style='font-size:14.0pt'>Evaluate (0.75 hour)</span></font></a></h1>

<p class=MsoNormal><font size=2 face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB style='font-size:
11.0pt'>An  appropriate summary view of each team’s design is selected by the
team to be shared and evaluated. The summary view to be used will depend on ‘X’
(e.g. if ‘X’ is Collaborative Learning the appropriate summary view will be
generated by Web Collage).</span></font></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><font size=2 face=Arial><span
lang=EN-GB style='font-size:11.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></font></p>

<p class=MsoCommentText><font size=2 face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:10.0pt'>Designs are swapped amongst each group and evaluated
heuristically (<a
href="http://www.ld-grid.org/resources/methods-and-methodologies/heuristic-evaluation">http://www.ld-grid.org/resources/methods-and-methodologies/heuristic-evaluation</a><span
class=MsoHyperlink><u><font color=blue face=Arial><span style='font-family:
"Arial","sans-serif"'>). </span></font></u></span></span></font></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><font size=2 face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB style='font-size:
11.0pt'>Facilitators and critical friends advise on how to apply the teams
evaluation checklist to the design in question.</span></font></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><font size=2 face=Arial><span
lang=EN-GB style='font-size:11.0pt'>The final step is to share the result of
the evaluation back to group as a whole.</span></font></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><font size=2 face=Arial><span
lang=EN-GB style='font-size:11.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></font></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><b><font size=2 face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:11.0pt;font-weight:bold'>Resources</span></font></b></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i><font size=2 face=Arial><span
lang=EN-GB style='font-size:11.0pt;font-style:italic'>Participants’ resources:
the design of another team, along with the same team’s evaluation checklist
(i.e. the output from Activity 4).  </span></font></i></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><font size=2 face=Arial><span
lang=EN-GB style='font-size:11.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></font></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><font size=2 face=Arial><span
lang=EN-GB style='font-size:11.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></font></p>

<h1><a name="_Toc355954539"><b><font size=4 face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:14.0pt'>10<font size=1 face="Times New Roman"><span
style='font:7.0pt "Times New Roman"'>&nbsp;&nbsp; </span></font></span></font></b><font
size=4><span lang=EN-GB style='font-size:14.0pt'>Reflect (0.5 hour)</span></font></a></h1>

<p class=MsoNormal><font size=2 face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB style='font-size:
11.0pt'>In the last activity (‘Evaluate’), each group’s design was evaluated by
another group, and the results of the evaluation shared. Now everyone should
have an understanding of all the designs. </span></font></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><font size=2 face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB style='font-size:
11.0pt'>Participant groups should now reflect on if and how their activity
design could be connected to other groups designs to produce a course. </span></font></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><font size=2 face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB style='font-size:
11.0pt'>&nbsp;</span></font></p>

style='font-size:14.0pt'>11<font size=1 face="Times New Roman"><span
style='font:7.0pt "Times New Roman"'>&nbsp;&nbsp; </span></font></span></font></b><font
size=4><span lang=EN-GB style='font-size:14.0pt'>Wrap up (0.25 hour)</span></font></a></h1>

<p class=MsoNormal><font size=2 face=Arial><span lang=EN-GB style='font-size:
11.0pt'>The facilitators conclude by describing how participants can find out
more about learning design in general, and the ILDE in particular.</span></font></p>
