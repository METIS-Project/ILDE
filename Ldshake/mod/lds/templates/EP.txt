<p class="MsoNormal" style="text-align:justify"><i><span lang="EN-GB">Please use the
patterns template below to extract a pattern from the methodology design
narratives authored by the METIS partners.&nbsp;
This is meant to be a simple task where you read a partner’s design
narrative and you think about what the activity reminds you of in regards to an
activity you completed.&nbsp; The goal is to
understand and articulate the similarity between the two activities.&nbsp; The similarity may not necessarily capture
everything about the activity, but it does capture a design element.&nbsp; To facilitate this we are providing the
template below where you can articulate what is similar about the context,
problem and method of solution.&nbsp; <o:p></o:p></span></i></p>

cross-review spreadsheet</i></a></span><i><span lang="EN-GB"> to indicate whose
design narrative you have reviewed, the pattern (name) and any notes by <b>22<sup>nd</sup> of May 2013</b>. Then

<ol style="margin-top:0cm" start="2" type="1">
 <li class="MsoNormal" style="text-align:justify;mso-list:l0 level1 lfo1"><b><span lang="EN-GB">Problem<o:p></o:p></span></b></li>
</ol>

<p class="MsoNormal" style="text-align:justify"><b><span lang="EN-GB">&nbsp;</span></b></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style="text-align:justify"><i><span lang="EN-GB">The problem description provides a clear and
succinct presentation of the motivation for the pattern, its raison d'être.<o:p></o:p></span></i></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style="text-align:justify"><i><span lang="EN-GB">&nbsp;</span></i></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style="text-align:justify"><i><span lang="EN-GB">The educational research literature tends to
shy away from the term “problem”, as it may suggest a negative attitude. This
thesis takes the position of the design literature, and specifically the
computer science tradition, were problems are seen as opportunities for action.<o:p></o:p></span></i></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style="text-align:justify"><i><span lang="EN-GB">&nbsp;</span></i></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style="text-align:justify"><i><span lang="EN-GB">From a design perspective, where there is no
problem, nothing needs to be changed, no act of design is required and
consequently there are no questions for research.<o:p></o:p></span></i></p>

<ol style="margin-top:0cm" start="4" type="1">
 <li class="MsoNormal" style="text-align:justify;mso-list:l0 level1 lfo1"><b><span lang="EN-GB">Method of solution<o:p></o:p></span></b></li>
</ol>

<p class="MsoNormal" style="text-align:justify"><b><span lang="EN-GB">&nbsp;</span></b></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style="text-align:justify"><i><span lang="EN-GB">The solution is the centrepiece of the
pattern. In scientific terms, it is the claim that under certain conditions the
described actions will have a particular effect which addresses the problem.</span></i><i><span lang="EN-GB"><o:p></o:p></span></i></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style="text-align:justify"><i><span lang="EN-GB">The solution would ideally be articulated at
a level of detail which allows immediate implementation, and yet is applicable
beyond the specific experiences from which it is described. However, in a
hierarchy of patterns, several levels of abstraction will be represented and
the respective solutions will be positioned appropriately.</span></i><i><span lang="EN-GB"><o:p></o:p></span></i></p>
