Written report review
Problem:

Students need to show the work they have carried out during collaborative activities, and present the results of it.

Solution:

Through written reports, students have the chance to describe and present their work. By assessing written reports, it is not only possible to grade students, but also the teacher can give feedback to the students, indicating the strong and weak points of the report. On the other hand, reports can be serve to share the work with other students, which can be used to prepare further collaborative activities.

Application guidelines:

In order to include this assessment technique, is is recommended:

  • This pattern defines the assessment technique used. Therefore, it cannot be combined with any other pattern that also defines an assessment technique. However, patterns that propose some other additional configuration can be combined with this one.
  • In the assessed activity, students are expected to complete a report, to be assessed. Therefore, instructions about this task should be included in this activity.
  • In the assess task, the assessor needs instructions about how to assess the reports (for instance, you can include Rubrics in this activity).
  • Additionally, it is necessary to make sure that the assessor will have access to the reports to assess (in the assess task), especially if these are electronic resources.
