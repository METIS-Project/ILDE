Random group member assessment
Problem:

In collaborative work, it may happen that the distribution of tasks within a group makes it difficult for all the members to understand the work carried out by all the members, or even the global solution. On the other hand, there is also a risk that some group members do not contribute with their own share, and relies in their partners to complete the assigned work.

Solution:

In these cases, individual accountability needs to be fostered among all members in each group. A possibility to achieve this is to propose an assessment that only one member of each group will take. This member will be chosen randomly, and the grade achieved will be given to all members in their groups.

By means of this type of assessment, all students are aware of their responsibility towards the group. Additionally, they will have an interest in making sure that all group members reach the learning objectives, so that they can achieve a good grade. Thus, students are encourage to help their partners participate and understand the covered topics or problems.

Application guidelines:

To apply this pattern in an assessment, please consider:

  • This pattern does not define the assessment technique used, only an additional aspect of the configuration of the assessment. Therefore, it can be used with other patterns that define the assessment technique.
  • In the assessed activity, students must be aware of the way the assessment is going to be conducted. knowing the type of assessment parpares them to face the assessment, and increases the chances that the assessment will have the aforementioned benefits on learning.
  • In the assess task, instructions should be given to the assessor about how to conduct the assessment.
  • It would be adequate to indicate that one of the usages of the assessment results is "to grade students".
  • This pattern can be considered as a particular case of Shared grades.
