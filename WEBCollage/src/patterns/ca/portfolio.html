Portfolio assessment
Problem:

How can we assess the achievements and development of students over a period of time?

Solution:

Portfolio assessment has been proposed as an alternative form of assessment, attempting to gather together evidentes of the achievements and progress of the students. Students' works, along with their reflections on them, can serve as a source of information about the competencies, knowledge and abilities, possibly more reliable than other means of assessment.

Additionally, a portfolio compiled by a student can show not only their learning achievements, but also the learning process, giving information about how the competencies have been developed.

Finally, a portfolio is adequate to follow long-term projects, and it gives a context for the revision, guidance and critique of students' work.

Application guidelines:

In order to include this assessment technique, is is recommended:

  • This pattern defines the assessment technique used. Therefore, it cannot be combined with any other pattern that also defines an assessment technique. However, patterns that propose some other additional configuration can be combined with this one.
  • The portfolio is compiled over a period of time. Therefore, there can be several activities in which students complete the porfolio (or this can be a resource present in all the learning activities).
  • If a script has a long duration (for instance, a whole course), there can exist several assessments of the same portfolio, at different moments. These would have to be created independently in Web Collage.
  • In the assessed activity, students are expected to compile and prepare for evaluation their portfolios. Therefore, the corresponding instructions should be included in this activity.
  • The assessor reviews the portfolios in the assessed activity. Therefore, instructions should be given as to how accomplish this task. Also, it is necessary to make sure that the assessor will have access to the portfolios, if these are electronic resources.
