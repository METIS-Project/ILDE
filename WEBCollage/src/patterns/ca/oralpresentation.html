Oral oresentation
Problem:

Work produced in groups needs to be explained and defended by the students, in front of the teacher and the rest of students.

Solution:

Oral presentations give a chance to students to show and defend the work they have carried out. Through this technique, it is possible to assess whether the students are capable of explaining and argumenting their work. Additionally, an oral presentation give the chance to look deeper into the understanding of the student, if questions are allowed. Finally, presentations can be used to have all the students listen to other groups' work, and in this way prepare richer discussions.

Application guidelines:

In order to include this assessment technique, is is recommended:

  • This pattern defines the assessment technique used. Therefore, it cannot be combined with any other pattern that also defines an assessment technique. However, patterns that propose some other additional configuration can be combined with this one.
  • Instructions should be included in the assessed activity: this is the activity where students perform the presentation.
  • In the assess task, instructions should be included for the assessor. For instance, you can include a Rubric, to clarify the assessment criteria.
  • It is necessary to make sure that the assessor will have access to the presentation. For instance, the assess task should happen at the same time as the assessed activity (the presentation itself). An alternative to this is to provide the assessor with a recording of the presentation instead (this could be a resource included in the assess task).
