Shared grades
Problem:

Assessment and grading may bring students to the conclusion that it is not worthy to put effort in collaborative work, for instance if their grades depend only on their own individual work.

Solution:

In these cases, possitive interdependence should be fostered, that is, make that all group members are necessary to achieve a goal. This goal may be related to the grade that the students are given.

By making that part of the students' grade is shared among all group members, possitive interdependece can be fostered, if the reward that each student receives depends on other students.

One way of applying this patterns is to make the students' grade dependent on their partners' achievements. For instance, certain part of the grade can be given only if all group members achieve certain learning objective. Another example of this pattern can be found in Random group member assessment.

Application guidelines:

To apply this pattern, please consider:

  • This pattern does not define the assessment technique used, only an additional aspect of the configuration of the assessment. Therefore, it can be used with other patterns that define the assessment technique.
  • In the assessed activity, students must be aware of the way the assessment is going to be conducted. knowing the type of assessment parpares them to face the assessment, and increases the chances that the assessment will have the aforementioned benefits on learning.
  • In the assess task, instructions should be given to the assessor about how to conduct the assessment.
  • It would be adequate to indicate that one of the usages of the assessment results is "to grade students".
