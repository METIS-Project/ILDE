Performance assessment
Problem:

How can we assess the students' ability to apply knowledge and skills to situation similar to those that they will find in the 'real world'?

Solution:

Prepare activities that put students in situations similar to those they will find in their professional career; assess the application of knowledge, skills and abilities in those situations.

This type of assessment can focus on the products that the task is expected to produce; on the other hand, it is also possible to observe the procedures and bahevior of the students, in order to gather information about their knowledge and skills.

Performance assessment can be organized as a simulation, reflecting typical situations of the work environment in which the students will find themselves. On the other hand, software and hardware systems can be used to aid the simulations: in some cases, systems as sofisticated as flight simulators, or simulations of laboratory equipment can be used.

Other examples of performance assessment can include more traditional methods: Review of written report can be used to measure writing skills. An assessment of an Oral presentation can also serve to assess the students' public speaking skills.

Application guidelines:

In order to include this assessment technique, is is recommended:

  • This pattern defines the assessment technique used. Therefore, it cannot be combined with any other pattern that also defines an assessment technique. However, patterns that propose some other additional configuration can be combined with this one.
  • Instructions should be included in the assessed activity: this is the activity where students perform the assigned task.
  • In the assess task, instructions should be included for the assessor. For instance, you can include a Rubric, to clarify the assessment criteria.
  • It is necessary to make sure that the assessor will have access to the presentation. For instance, the assess task should happen at the same time as the assessed activity (the students' performance itself). An alternative to this is to provide the assessor with a recording of the performance instead (this could be a resource included in the assess task).
