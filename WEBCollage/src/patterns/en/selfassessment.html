Self-assessment
Problem:

In collaborative learning, it is important that groups and students are capable of managing their own organization of group work, and if keeping effective work relationships. To achieve this, they need to be aware of the way they are working, and the problems they might have.

Solution:

To avoid that groups of students stop working effectively, it is a good idea to reserve some time for them to self-assess their own work. By analyzing their own performance and problems in their work, the students can identify positive and negative actions, and can take decisions to correct the existing problems.

In order to engage students in this self-assessment task, explicit activities should be created, indicating clearly their objective and purpose.

By means of self-assessment, not only can students work more efficiently, but also feedback given to their group partners can improve their learning of collaboration abilities.

Application guidelines:

To set a self-assessment in a script, it is recommended:

  • This pattern does not define the assessment technique used, only an additional aspect of the configuration of the assessment. Therefore, it can be used with other patterns that define the assessment technique.
  • Since the assessor and assessee are the same person or group, it may be adequate to check that the assessed activity and the assess task are assigned to the same role (this is not mandatory though). In any case, the assessors should be the students.
  • In the assess task, instructions should be given for the assessor, indicating the purpose of the assessment, according to the aforementioned potential benefits of self-assessment.
